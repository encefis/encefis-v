							<h3 class="title mb-3">Materiales Porosos Irreversibles Saturados. Modelos y Aplicaciones</h3>
							<div class="desc"><p align="justify">El desarrollo científico en el estudio de las propriedades de geomateriales ha permitido la incorporación de nuevos procedimientos tecnológicos para alcanzar mejores niveles de optimización en la explotación de recursos naturales. Las formaciones geológicas son constituidas por materiales porosos, los cuales son permeados por el flujo de fluidos de diversos tipos. Aquíferos, reservatorios de gas o hidrocarbonatos junto con la matriz rocosa constituyen ejemplos de cuerpos porosos saturados, cuyo comportamiento es gobernado por el acoplamiento de las leyes físicas típicas de las escalas porosa y geología. Las interacciones entre la matriz porosa (fase solida) y las fases fluidas pueden alcanzar a situaciones de bloqueo o abertura de caminos, que por su vez, pueden conducir a alteraciones de la poro-presión afectando en la deformación irreversible de la camada geológica. La complexidad de los fenómenos geomecánicas es incrementada si considerada la alta heterogeneidad de las características y propriedades físicas de los materiales envolvidos, siendo necesaria la incorporación de modelos probabilísticos. En esta presentación, abordaremos la formulación de Biot, conocida como poromecánica, que tiene como base la termodinámica de medios continuos. Mostraremos las leyes físicas y resultantes expresiones matemáticas que dictan el comportamiento irreversible de materiales porosos saturados. Finalmente, mostraremos los procedimientos numéricos de simulación computacional para el caso de la explotación de petróleo en la región del pre-sal Brasileño, localizado entre 5 a 7 mil metros de profundidad al nivel del mar.</p></div>

							<h3 class="title mb-3">Two-dimensional tungsten disulfide nanosheets and their application in self-powered photodetectors with ultra-high sensitivity and stability</h3>
							<div class="desc"><p align="justify">Two-dimensional (2D) tungsten disulfide nanosheets (WS2) could be a promising candidate for high-performance self-powered photodetectors. The present 2D nanosheets were obtained from liquid exfoliation in a mixture of ethanol, methanol, and isopropanol via a direct dispersion and ultrasonication method. Using the spin-coating technique, a thin film of uniform thickness was formed on the SiO2/Si substrate. Energy-dispersive X-ray analysis showed that the S/W ratio in the fabricated WS2 film was around 1.2 to 1.34, indicating certain deficiencies in the S atoms. These S vacancies induce localized states within the bandgap of pristine WS2, resulting in a higher conductivity in the exfoliated sample. The obtained thin film seems to be highly efficient in photoelectric conversion, with a responsivity of ~ 0.12 mA/W at 670 nm under zero bias voltage, with an intensity of 5.2 mW/cm2. Instead, at a bias of 2 V, it exhibits a responsivity of 12.74 mA/W and a detectivity of 1.17 × 1010 cm Hz1/2 W-1 at 4.1 mW/cm2. The present 2D nanosheets exhibit high photon absorption in a wide range of spectra from the near-infrared (IR) to near UV spectrum.</p></div>

							<h3 class="title mb-3">Arecibo confirms infrared ZoA galaxies selected from spitzer data</h3>
							<div class="desc"><p align="justify">We report the detection of 7 new galaxies in HI in the deepest 
                            regions of the Zone of Avoidance using the Arecibo Telescope. The detected galaxies are part of a sample of 25 sources, 
                            identified as galaxies using IR images from the SPITZER Space Telescope by Marleau et al 2008. The sample is composed of 
                            disk galaxies as it was one of the source identification criteria in their survey. None of the sources have optical counterparts. 
                            The velocity of the new galaxies in HI are between 5,700 km s-1 and 10,900 km s-1 and their estimated masses range from 1.5 x 10⁹ to 1.3 x 1010 solar masses. 
                            Although the studied region is part of two HI blind searches, none of our sources were detected in HI previously. 
                            Blind searches are optimized to cover large areas of sky but are not very deep. 
                            Due to the HI intensities of the detected galaxies, none of them could be detected in this type of search. 
                            HI observations of specific candidates allows more in-depth observations than blind searches but requires candidate lists to be established. 
                            That is not possible in optical for the ZoA. 
                            The results of this work shows that the identification of galaxies in the ZoA using IR data such as that of Marleau et al. 2008 
                            is a viable way to study the deepest areas of the ZoA, providing candidates for further HI observations. Motivated by these results, 
                            we have continued to work with the SPITZER Space Telescope archived data to identify new candidates in the ZoA. This work is in preparation.</p></div>
